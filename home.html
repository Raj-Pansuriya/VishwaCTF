FAQ

What is CTF?

CTF stands for "Capture the Flag". CTFs are a type of computer security competition (but HSCTF extends beyond computer security to include other areas of computer science). Certain pieces of information, called "flags", are placed on servers, encrypted, hidden, or otherwise stored somewhere difficult to access. During the competition, different challenges are released which allow the participants to reverse engineer, break, hack, decrypt, and do whatever it takes to capture that flag. When a team submits this flag to a scoring page, they will get points.


Who can compete?

Anyone and Everyone!!!


Where?

VishwaCTF will take place entirely online at this website. Once the competition starts, teams will be able to log in and view the challenges using the team name and password provided at registration, which will be open throughout the competition. Participants may find it useful to have the ability to install software and have unfiltered internet access.


Who is running VishwaCTF?

VishwaCTF is organized by students at Vishvakarma Institute of Information Technology in Pune as part of its Cyber Cell.
